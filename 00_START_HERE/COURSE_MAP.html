Course Map — CS 6515 Intro to Graduate Algorithms (Summer 2026)

High-level mental model of the course. For the precise week/reading/exam table, see INDEX.md; for routing logic, AGENT_ROUTING.md.

The five-and-a-half big units

  1. Dynamic Programming (DP) — break a problem into a table of overlapping subproblems; define a recurrence; fill bottom-up. Weeks 1, 4.
  2. Divide & Conquer (DC) — split, recurse, combine; analyze with the Master theorem. Weeks 2, 3 (FFT in Week 11).
  3. Graphs (GR) — DFS/BFS as the foundation; SCCs, 2-SAT, MST. Weeks 4, 5.
  4. Max-Flow (MF) — flows, residual graphs, max-flow/min-cut; Edmonds-Karp. Weeks 6, 7.
  5. NP-Completeness (NP) — reductions, 3-SAT, hardness; coping (approximation). Weeks 8, 10.
  6. Linear Programming (LP) — geometry, duality, LP-relaxation/approximation. Weeks 9, 10.

Plus a Week-11 advanced grab-bag: FFT, flow applications, number theory / RSA, Bloom filters.

Key throughlines

  • DFS (Week 4) is the linchpin: SCCs, 2-SAT, topological sort, and cycle detection all fall out of pre/post numbering.
  • Max-flow (Week 6–7) is the bridge from graphs into LP — max-flow/min-cut is the first LP-duality example you meet.
  • NP-completeness (Week 8) reframes everything: which of these problems are easy, and what do you do (approximate, LP-relax) when a problem is hard (Week 10)?

Exam rhythm

Three exams, each closing a unit block: Exam 1 after DP+DC (W1–3), Exam 2 after graphs+flow (W4–7), Exam 3 after NP+LP (W8–10). Week 11 is cumulative review (Quiz 8). See the Exam Coverage Map in INDEX.md.
